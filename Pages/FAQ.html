Frequently Asked Questions

If you have something else we can help with, reach out to us.

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where people come together to work on coding projects, usually in a collaborative and competitive environment.

What kind of projects can I work on?

You can work on any coding project you'd like, as long as it's within the scope of the event. We encourage participants to be creative and innovative, and we'll have mentors available to help with any technical issues.

Do I need to be an experienced coder?

We recommend having a base level understanding of at least one programming language. It's okay not to be the best programmer, you can still have impact!

What kind of support is available?

We'll have mentors available to provide technical guidance and support throughout the event. We'll also provide workshops and talks to help participants develop their skills and learn new things.

Do I need to come with a team?

No, you can either come with a team or join a team on the day of the event. We'll also have team-building activities to help participants find collaborators.

Are there prizes to be won?

We'll be offering a range of prizes for top-performing teams, including cash prizes, sponsored prizes, and opportunities for mentorship and networking.

What should I bring to the hackathon?

You should bring your own laptop and any necessary chargers or other equipment you'll need to work on your project. We'll provide the rest, including food and drinks.

How do I register for the hackathon?

You can register for the hackathon through our website here. Simply follow the link to the UQ ticketing page.
